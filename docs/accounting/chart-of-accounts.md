# Chart of Accounts

The chart of accounts is the foundation of your accounting system. It organizes your financial accounts into groups and provides the structure for all transactions.

## Account Groups

Ballie uses five main account groups:

| Group | Type | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Assets | Debit | What your business owns (cash, inventory, equipment) |
| Liabilities | Credit | What your business owes (loans, payables) |
| Equity | Credit | Owner's investment and retained earnings |
| Revenue | Credit | Income from sales and services |
| Expenses | Debit | Costs of running your business |

## Managing Ledger Accounts

### Viewing Accounts

Navigate to **Accounting → Chart of Accounts** to see all your ledger accounts organized by group.

### Creating a New Account

1. Click **+ New Account**
2. Select the **Account Group** (e.g., Assets, Expenses)
3. Enter the **Account Name** and optional **Account Code**
4. Set the **Opening Balance** if applicable
5. Click **Save**

### Editing an Account

1. Click on any account in the list
2. Modify the name, code, or other details
3. Click **Update**

::: warning
Deleting an account with existing transactions is not allowed. Disable it instead.
:::

## Default Chart of Accounts

During onboarding, Ballie sets up a default chart of accounts with common accounts for Nigerian businesses, including:
- Cash and bank accounts
- Accounts receivable and payable
- Sales revenue and cost of goods sold
- Common expense categories
